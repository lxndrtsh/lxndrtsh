Version Control

git pull origin master

I've been a firm believer in making life as easy as possible when coding. I find that good developers are lazy and will ignore even the best of practices if it's not simple to follow. What makes version control incredible is the ease of use it has for developers to actively maintain a codebase while keeping other teammates in the loop.

Where I work currently, we actively deploy using a dev > QA > RC > master practice that allows us to do constant deployments and active development.

I have worked with Github, Bitbucket and Crucible - along with Jenkins and Bamboo for auto deployment. As a software engineer manager, I am handling large amounts of work that goes through our version control system while handling releases on a weekly/bi-weekly sprint.

I've also got good experience with Subversion, Mercurial, and TFS!

Team Management

My current job has given me a lot of responsibility to help manage teams of developers who are creating a wide range of different products for the clients. I am actively using team management software and practices. Some of my current toosl include JIRA, with all of its glory.

My main goal as a manager to help eliminate any roadblocks for my team! Every morning we have a daily stand-up where my team mates tell me what they worked on yesterday, what they are working on today, as well as if there any roadblocks. I will put off other tasks in order to remove these roadblocks!

I take pride in managing the fine balance between the business side and the engineers; managers are trusted protectors of the engineer's interest who will negotiate with the business side on requirements and expectations. I may be a goof-ball, but that is one area of my job that I take completely seriously.
